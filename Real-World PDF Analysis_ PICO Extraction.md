# Real-World PDF Analysis: PICO Extraction

## Study Information
**Title:** Adaptive deep brain stimulation for Parkinson's disease demonstrates reduced speech side effects compared to conventional stimulation in the acute setting

**Journal:** Journal of Neurology, Neurosurgery & Psychiatry (JNNP)
**Year:** 2016
**Study Type:** Randomized Controlled Trial (Crossover Design)

## PICO Analysis

### Population (P)
- **Target Population:** Patients with advanced idiopathic Parkinson's disease
- **Sample Size:** 10 patients recruited, 8 completed (2 excluded due to technical failure)
- **Specific Criteria:** 
  - Advanced idiopathic PD
  - Following implantation of DBS electrodes into the subthalamic nucleus (STN)
  - Tested 3-6 days following electrode placement during temporary externalization period
  - Overnight withdrawal of dopaminergic medication

### Intervention (I)
- **Primary Intervention:** Adaptive Deep Brain Stimulation (aDBS)
- **Mechanism:** Stimulation automatically adjusted according to β oscillatory activity (12-30 Hz biomarker)
- **Delivery:** Bilateral stimulation delivered only when β amplitude exceeded threshold
- **Parameters:** Mean voltage 2.7±0.2 V, stimulation delivered 42.6±3.7% of the time
- **Duration:** 15 minutes per stimulation block

### Comparator (C)
- **Control 1:** Conventional Deep Brain Stimulation (cDBS) - continuous stimulation
- **Control 2:** OFF DBS condition (no stimulation)
- **Design:** Blinded and randomized crossover comparison
- **Parameters:** Same contacts/voltages used for both aDBS and cDBS conditions

### Outcomes (O)
**Primary Outcomes:**
1. **Speech Intelligibility:** Measured using standardized Speech Intelligibility Test (SIT)
   - Participants read sentences totaling 110 words
   - % intelligibility assessed by blinded speech and language therapist
   
2. **Motor Function:** MDS-UPDRS-III assessment (6 of 8 participants)
   - Video-recorded and rated offline by two blinded movement disorder specialists
   - Rigidity excluded (vUPDRS total=112)

**Key Results:**
- Speech intelligibility: aDBS (70.4±6.4%) vs cDBS (60.5±8.2%) - statistically significant improvement
- Motor scores: aDBS (19.7±1.0) vs cDBS (31.6±4.3) - significant improvement with aDBS
- aDBS showed 9.9% improvement in speech intelligibility compared to cDBS

## Inclusion Criteria (Extracted)
1. Advanced idiopathic Parkinson's disease
2. Implanted with DBS electrodes in subthalamic nucleus (STN)
3. Able to provide informed written consent
4. Available for testing 3-6 days post-electrode placement
5. Able to tolerate overnight withdrawal of dopaminergic medication

## Exclusion Criteria (Extracted)
1. External stimulator failure preventing voltage delivery
2. Inability to complete assessments due to fatigue/discomfort
3. Non-idiopathic Parkinson's disease (implied)

## Study Limitations (As Noted by Authors)
1. Acute nature in postoperative period with microlesional effect
2. Responses may not represent chronic state
3. Time constraints and patient fatigue
4. Short stimulation blocks may have lessened mean effect
5. Limited sample size (n=8)
6. Blinded video-based assessments may have obscured some cDBS benefits

## Clinical Significance
- First blinded group data showing aDBS potential for better efficacy with fewer side effects
- 9.9% improvement in speech intelligibility clinically relevant
- Suggests aDBS may have wider therapeutic window than conventional DBS
- Proof of concept for reduced speech side effects with adaptive stimulation
